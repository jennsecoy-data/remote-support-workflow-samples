# Escalation Process Sample

This sample outlines a basic escalation workflow for email, chat, or help desk support.

## When to Escalate

Escalation may be needed when:

- The customer issue cannot be resolved with available information
- Account, billing, order, or policy details require supervisor review
- The customer reports repeated unresolved issues
- The request involves missing, delayed, or conflicting information
- The customer is upset and needs additional support
- The issue may affect customer satisfaction or service quality

## Information to Document Before Escalation

Before escalating, the support agent should document:

- Customer name or account reference
- Ticket or order number
- Contact method
- Summary of the customer’s issue
- Steps already taken
- Information provided to the customer
- Missing or unclear details
- Reason escalation is needed
- Recommended next step

## Example Escalation Note

Customer contacted support through live chat regarding a delayed order. Order status was reviewed, but tracking information was unavailable. Customer was informed that the issue would be escalated for additional review. Ticket includes order number, customer concern, current status, and follow-up need.

## Escalation Status

- Issue reviewed: Yes
- Customer updated: Yes
- Escalation reason documented: Yes
- Follow-up needed: Yes
- Ticket status: Pending review
