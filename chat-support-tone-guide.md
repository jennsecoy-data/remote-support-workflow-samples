# Chat Support Tone Guide

This sample guide outlines tone and communication standards for live chat and email support.

## Tone Standards

Customer support responses should be:

- Clear
- Professional
- Calm
- Helpful
- Respectful
- Easy to understand
- Focused on the customer’s issue

## Best Practices

- Acknowledge the customer’s concern
- Use simple, direct language
- Avoid blaming the customer
- Avoid overpromising
- Provide the next step clearly
- Keep the response organized
- Confirm what was reviewed or completed
- Document the interaction accurately

## Example: Strong Response

Hi [Customer Name],

Thank you for reaching out. I reviewed the order information and confirmed that the order is currently delayed in transit. The tracking information shows the latest update on [date].

I documented the issue and recommend checking for another carrier update within 24 hours. Please let us know if the tracking does not update or if you need additional help.

## Example: Response Needing Improvement

Your package is delayed. Check tracking later.

## Why It Needs Improvement

- Tone is too brief
- Does not acknowledge the customer’s concern
- Does not explain what was reviewed
- Does not provide a helpful next step
- Does not sound customer-focused
